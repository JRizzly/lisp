Summer Ends and Fall Begins

August 23rd is the official first day of Autumn. Leaves will begin to fall and temperatures will start to cool. Here are a few tips to keep up with your lawn in the transition:

  • Rake your lawn regularly to avoid suffocation (of your lawn) by dead leaves and branches.
  • Create a compost pile with your dead leaves/flowers, grass clippings, plant-based kitchen waste, barnyard manure (do not use manure from carnivores, such as dogs or cats), and a shovelful or two of garden soil. You want the mixture to be approximately 3 parts brown (fall leaves, straw, and dead flowers) to 1 part green (grass clippings, plant-based kitchen waste, and manure) and you want to keep the pile moist. Every couple of weeks, using a shovel or garden fork, turn the pile. Keep adding to your pile until it is approximately 3x3x3. Eventually the pile will be completely decomposed and turn to a black crumbly compost. This is wonderful for layering onto your fruit/vegetable garden, flowerbeds, and lawn!
  • Keep your grass fertilized and watered to help prepare for harsh winter days in the future!
  • This is the time to raise the setting on your mower and allow the lawn to grow a little taller. This will help keep your grass strong and healthy for the cold weather to come.
